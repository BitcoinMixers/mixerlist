## What Is a Bitcoin Mixer?

A **Bitcoin mixer** (also referred to as a Bitcoin tumbler) is a service aimed at improving privacy on the Bitcoin network. It works by obscuring the connection between sending and receiving Bitcoin addresses, helping users protect their identities.

Mixers operate by pooling funds from multiple users, redistributing them, and applying time delays and other methods to break the traceability of transactions.

---

## How Bitcoin Mixers Work

Bitcoin mixers work by following these general steps:

1. Bitcoin is sent to an address controlled by the mixer
2. The funds are pooled together with other Bitcoin transactions
3. The coins are redistributed to new addresses to ensure privacy
4. Optional features such as time delays and output variations are added to further protect user privacy

This process enhances privacy by making it difficult to trace the origin and destination of the funds on the blockchain.

## Bitcoin Mixers and CoinJoin

**CoinJoin** is a powerful privacy technique often used alongside Bitcoin mixers. Unlike traditional mixers, CoinJoin involves merging multiple Bitcoin transactions into a single transaction, which helps mask the identities of the participants.

Both **CoinJoin** and Bitcoin mixers are crucial tools for users who want to secure their privacy and make Bitcoin transactions harder to trace. These tools remain relevant in 2026 as blockchain analysis techniques evolve.
